One of the main reasons why I believe in preserving materials that might be deemed offensive is because censorship limits our freedom of expression and access to information. This goes against the principles of intellectual freedom and diversity of thought. While some of these works may have been controversial or uncomfortable to read, they have ultimately enriched my perspective and broadened my horizons. Libraries should serve as spaces that encourage open dialogue and intellectual exploration, rather than gatekeepers of what is deemed acceptable or unacceptable.  In conclusion, I firmly believe that censorship in libraries is detrimental to the principles of intellectual freedom and diversity. By preserving access to a wide range of materials, even those that may be considered offensive, we can foster a more inclusive and intellectually stimulating environment for all individuals.
