They are typically found in public places such as airports, schools, and recreational facilities, and are also commonly used in ambulances and hospitals.In order to use a defibrillator, it is important to have received proper training and to follow the instructions provided by the device. The defibrillator will typically provide voice prompts to guide the user through the process of administering the shock. It is important to only use a defibrillator when it is indicated, as inappropriate use can cause harm to the patient.If you are trained in the use of a defibrillator and you encounter someone who is unconscious and not breathing, you should immediately call emergency medical services (EMS) and follow the instructions provided by the defibrillator to administer a shock if indicated. It is important to act quickly in these situations, as the chances of survival decrease significantly with each passing minute.

Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) is a virus that attacks the immune system, the body's defense system against infections and diseases. HIV causes acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS), a condition in which the immune system becomes severely weakened and is unable to fight off infections and diseases. AIDS is a serious and potentially life-threatening condition that requires medical treatment.HIV is transmitted through bodily fluids such as blood, semen, vaginal secretions, and breast milk. HIV can be transmitted through sexual contact, injection drug use, and mother-to-child transmission during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding. HIV cannot be transmitted through casual contact such as shaking hands, hugging, or sharing food and drinks.There is no cure for HIV, but antiretroviral therapy (ART) can help people with HIV to manage the virus and live long and healthy lives. ART involves taking a combination of medications called antiretroviral drugs, which can suppress the virus and help to rebuild the immune system. With proper treatment and care, people with HIV can live normal, healthy lives and prevent transmission to others.
