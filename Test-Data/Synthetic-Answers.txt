More and more people use computers, but not everyone agrees that this benefits society. Those who support advances in technology believe that computers have a positive effect on people. They teach hand-eye coordination, give people the ability to learn about faraway places and people, and even allow people to talk online with other people. Others have different ideas. Write a letter to your local newspaper in which you state your opinion on the effects computers have on people. Persuade the readers to agree with you.

Dear Editor,

As a high school student, I have had the opportunity to grow up with technology and see the impact it has had on my life and the lives of those around me. While some people argue that computers have a negative effect on society, I firmly believe that they have had a positive impact.

First and foremost, computers have revolutionized the way we learn. With the internet at our fingertips, we have access to an infinite amount of information and resources. We can learn about faraway places and cultures, and connect with people from all over the world. This not only broadens our horizons, but also helps us to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Furthermore, computers have greatly improved our ability to communicate with one another. Email, social media, and video conferencing have made it easier than ever to stay in touch with friends and family who live far away. Online communities have also given people a platform to connect with others who share similar interests and passions.

In addition to these benefits, computers have also had a positive impact on our physical health. While it is true that some people spend too much time sitting in front of a screen, technology has also enabled us to stay active and healthy in new and innovative ways. For example, there are countless fitness apps and websites that provide exercise routines and tracking tools, as well as virtual reality games that encourage us to move and be active.

Finally, I would like to address the concern that computers are replacing human interaction. While it is true that some people may spend more time interacting with their devices than with other humans, technology has also given us the tools to connect with others in new and meaningful ways. Social media, online forums, and video conferencing have all made it easier for us to connect with others and build relationships, regardless of our geographical location.

In conclusion, computers have had a profoundly positive impact on our lives. They have given us access to information and resources, improved our ability to communicate with one another, enabled us to stay active and healthy, and provided us with new and innovative ways to connect with others. While there may be some downsides to our increasing reliance on technology, I believe that the benefits far outweigh the drawbacks. Thank you for reading.
